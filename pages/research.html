Research Overview

Explore our major research projects below.

BEFTA Project

The BEFTA project aims to quantify the effect of habitat complexity within oil palm plantations on biodiversity and the role of this biodiversity in ecosystem functioning and productivity. Working closely with industry partners, Sinar Mas Agro Resources and Technology Corporation Research Institute (SMARTRI), the project experimentally manipulates the oil palm landscape to link biodiversity changes with ecosystem functions and crop production.

  • Project objectives:
    • Quantify the effect of habitat complexity on biodiversity, ecosystem function, and services within oil palm plantations.
    • Develop experimental approaches to distinguish effects of habitat structure vs. biodiversity on ecosystem functioning.
    • Model optimal understory and epiphyte cover to maximize biodiversity and economic returns.
  • Experimental setup: 18 plots (2.25 ha each), 3 complexity levels, replicated 6 times in Riau, Sumatra.
  • Treatments:
    • Reduced complexity – removal of understory and epiphytes using herbicides.
    • Normal complexity – standard industry practice.
    • Enhanced complexity – reduced herbicide, minimal understory cutting.

Figure 1: BEFTA experimental design showing varying habitat complexity treatments.

Data Collection: We collect data on biodiversity, ecosystem function, crop yield and management. This is analyzed before and after treatment for accuracy.

RERTA Project

The Riparian Ecosystem Restoration in Tropical Agriculture (RERTA) Project, established in 2018, experimentally tests active vs. passive restoration in riparian margins during oil palm replanting.

  • Sampling locations:
    • Within the riparian margin (adjacent to the river)
    • 50m from the river
    • >500m into oil palm landscape
  • Treatments include:
    • A: Enrichment planting + mature oil palm buffer.
    • B: No restoration + mature oil palm buffer.
    • C: Enrichment planting + remove mature oil palm.
    • D: No restoration + replant young oil palm to river margin.

NERC El Niño Project

This NERC-funded project investigates how extreme weather (e.g. El Niño) affects ecosystem resilience in oil palm systems. The project uses BEFTA's experimental framework.

  • Studies how drought affects biodiversity, processes, and yield.
  • Assesses whether increased habitat complexity improves resilience and recovery after disturbance.
  • Findings inform sustainable management practices in oil palm production.
